SIR Model

Date Created: 3/26/2020

Code: Github

For modeling disease spread, the SIR model is a purposefully simple model. Let $S$ (blue) be the percentage of people currently susceptible to a virus. Let $I$ (red) be the percentage infected and let $R$ (green) be the percentage that has recovered (or died). $\alpha$ and $\beta$ are constants indicating the transmission and recovery rates of the disease. $I$ is initially small. This assumes that once someone recovers, they are permanently immune.

This model uses a set of differential equations to model the spread of this disease, namely $$ \begin{align*} S' &= -\alpha SI \\ I' &= \alpha SI - \beta I \\ R' &= \beta I \\ \end{align*} $$

I solved this with, what I now know, is Euler's method. If I did this again, I would use a better, more acurate method, like Runge-Kutta.
