Dice Game

Date Created: 4/28/2021

This is based on a fun dice game. Each player has two scores, initially set to zero. Let's call them the current score and banked score. On their turn, they roll a single die as many times as they want, each time adding the rolled value to their current score. Instead of rolling, they may bank their points in which case their current score is added to their banked score and their turn ends. However, if they roll a one, their turn ends without adding to their banked score.

What is a good strategy? One strategy is the player continue to roll until they reach some pre-defined goal. If they reach it (or exceed it), they end their turn. If not, roll again.

If your goal is too low, you will not earn enough points in a turn. If your goal is too high, it is unlikely you will earn any points in a turn. You could do some analysis to determine the optimal score or you could simulate it like I did. The blue bars are the current evaluation. The orange dots are the true values.
