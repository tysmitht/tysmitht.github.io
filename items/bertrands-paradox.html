Bertrands Paradox

Date Created: 12/26/2021

Code: Github

This is a demonstration of Bertrand's Paradox. Let $c$ be a random chord through a circle with length $\ell$. Inscribe an equilateral triangle in that circle and denote its side length as $s$. What then is $P(\ell > s)$?

By looking at a few different (but perfectly reasonable) methods for selecting $c$, we can get different probabilities. The point Bertrand is trying to make is that even if there is no bias in your method, you aren't guaranteed a well-defined result.
